Minerals

Overview

Why minerals? Minerals help your body grow, develop, and stay healthy. Minerals are used by the body to do a lot of things, such as building bones, helping provide energy for the body, and sending nerve impulses throughout the body.

There are two kinds of minerals: Macrominerals and trace minerals. Macro means "large" in Greek meaning that your body needs more of the macrominerals than trace minerals. Macrominerals include calcium, phosphorus, magnesium, sodium, potassium, chloride. Trace minerals include iron, manganese, copper, iodine, zinc.

Calcium

Strong bones and teeth! Calcium is such an important mineral to have in our bodies. Our nervous system, muscles, heart, and bones use calcium. Vitamin D is needed to help increase calcium absorption!

Sources of calcium include yogurt, milk, white beans, kale, cheese (softer cheeses tend to have less calcium than hard cheeses), broccoli, red beans, tofu, almonds.

Chromium

Needed in very small quantities, chromium can help increase muscle mass, weight loss, and improve blood sugar control by allowing the body to better use insulin.

Sources of chromium include broccoli, liver, brewer's yeast, potatoes, whole grains, seafood, and meats.

Copper

Copper works with iron to help make red blood cells. Copper also helps the body form collagen, an important protein that helps provide structure to hair, nails, skin, bones.

Sources of copper include oysters, whole grains, beans, potatoes, yeast, dark leafy greens, dried fruits, organ meats (kidneys, liver), and nuts (cashews, almonds).

Fluoride

Fluoride helps protect our teeth from decay. Fluoride also helps protect against bad bacteria in the mouth.

Sources of fluoride include drinking water, tea, coffee, rice, soybeans, spinach, onions, lettuce.

Iodine

Iodine is an essential mineral needed by your thyroid glands to produce thyroid hormones which have important responsibilities in your body like helping control the body's metabolism.

Sources of iodine include iodized salt, seaweed, cod, tuna, shrimp, eggs, and prunes.

Iron

Iron helps transport oxygen throughout the body! Iron also helps to keep your cells, skin, hair, and nails healthy.

Sources of iron include shellfish, spinach, legumes (beans, lentils, chickpeas, peas, soybeans), red meat, quinoa, turkey, broccoli, and tofu.

Magnesium

Magnesium is needed in more than 300 functions! Magnesium can help the body break down food, helps with communication throughout the body, and helps with improving our memory and learning!

Sources of magnesium include whole-grain cereals, tofu, nuts, meat, milk, green vegetables, legumes, chocolate.

Manganese

Manganese helps the body break down carbohydrates, amino acids, and fats. It can also be used in building bones.

Sources of manganese include blueberries, whole grains, nuts, legumes, fruit, tea.

Molybdenum

Molybdenum is used in the body to break down waste products and removing toxins in the body.

Sources of molybdenum include milk, cheese, legumes, cereal, grains, dark green leafy vegetables, organ meats.

Phosphorus

One of the most important minerals out there, phosphorus works with calcium to help build bones. Phosphorus is important in helping the body make energy.

Sources of phosphorus include meat, poultry, fish, eggs, nuts, legumes, cereals, grains, milk, cottage cheese.

Potassium

Potassium is an essential nutrient required for cells to function normally. Potassium is needed to help your heartbeat stay regular, helps with muscle movement, and helps with nerves to work.

Sources of potassium include legumes, potatoes, meats, fruits, vegetables, poultry, fish, milk, yogurt, nuts, brown rice.

Selenium

Selenium is a powerful antioxidant which can help prevent cell damage caused by free radicals. Selenium may help protect against heart disease, is important in keeping the thyroid healthy, and keeps the immune system healthy.

Sources of selenium include grains, onions, meats, milk.

Sodium

Sodium is an essential mineral in your body that helps maintain the balance of water in your body. Sodium also helps keep normal blood pressure levels, as well as helping for proper muscle movement and nerve function.

Common sources of sodium include salt, processed foods (deli meats, bacon, frozen pizza, soy sauce, hot dogs).

Zinc

Zinc plays a role in keeping a healthy immune system. Zinc also helps with cell growth and can help with wound healing.

Sources of zinc inlude oysters, beans, whole-grain cereals, nuts (peanuts, cashew), yogurt.

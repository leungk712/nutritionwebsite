Plant-Based Diets

What does a plant-based diet mean? A plant-based diet "...aims to maximize consumption of nutrient-dense plant foods while minimizing processed foods, oils, and animal foods (including dairy products and eggs)." A plant-based diet encourages lots of vegetables (cooked or raw), fruits, beans, peas, lentils, soybeans, seeds, and nuts.Research continues to show potential benefits towards the management of obesity, diabetes, heart disease, and life expectancy.

Diabetes

Diabetes is a huge problem all around the world. Diabetes occurs when your blood sugars are too high. This can cause a lot of problems such as tiredness, peeing a lot, weight loss or gain, feeling thirsty or hungry a lot, blurry vision and more. If diabetes is not controlled, it can increase your risk for a heart attack, stroke, blindness, nerve problems.

GERD

GERD occurs when your stomach acid flows back into your esophagus which can result in heartburn, chest pain, and trouble swallowing. GERD is usually treated with medication such as antacids, proton pump inhibitors, H2 blockers. Luckily, diet can help reduce the symptoms of GERD. These lists of food does not necessarily mean that you must avoid them all together, but it will help if you either eat these foods in small amounts or avoid it altogether.

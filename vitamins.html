Vitamin A

Vitamin A helps keep our teeth, bones, eyes, and skin healthy. Vitamin A helps with reproduction, helps keep cells working normally, and helps with growth!

Vitamin D

Vitamin D helps our body absorb calcium, an important mineral needed to keep our bones and teeth healthy. Vitamin D also helps with the immune system and can reduce inflammation. Vitamin D is not found in much foods, but can be added (fortified) in certain foods. The sun is the biggest way of getting Vitamin D! Get outside and soak up some sun!

Sources of Vitamin D
Salmon Tuna Cod Liver Oil Oysters Shrimp
Eggs Milk (fortified with Vitamin D) Orange Juice (fortified with Vitamin D) Ready-to-eat cereals (fortified with Vitamin D) Beef Liver

Vitamin E

Vitamin E is known as an important antioxidant in the body. Antioxidants help protect our body from free radicals which can damage and break down cells. Antioxidants can help protect us from diseases such as heart disease, cancer and other diseases.

Sources of Vitamin E
Sunflower seeds Almonds Hazelnuts Pine Nuts Peanuts
Atlantic Salmon Avocado Rainbow Trout Spinach Butternut Squash

Vitamin K

Vitamin K is an important vitamin that helps our blood clot as well as keep our bones strong and reduce our risk for health disease.

*If you are taking anti-coagulation medication (ex. Warfarin (Coumadin)), if you have not already, be sure to speak with your doctor on how much Vitamin K you should be eating each day!

Sources of Vitamin K
Kale Mustard Greens Swiss Chard Collard Greens Broccoli
Brussel Sprouts Beef Liver Pork Chops Chicken Parsley
